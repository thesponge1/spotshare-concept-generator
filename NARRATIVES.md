# Home Page Narrative Strategies

A working list of 16 different ways to tell SpotShare's story on the **Home page only**.
Resident, Property Manager, and HOA Board pages are out of scope for this list — those stay
on the existing audience-Preset system for now and will be revisited later, once a favorite
narrative has been picked here.

Unlike the section variants already in `src/data.ts`, these aren't interchangeable pieces —
each strategy is a complete, internally consistent retelling of the homepage. The 6 sections
stay the same (Hero, Problem, How It Works, Social Proof, Audience Router, Final CTA), but
each strategy redefines what job each section is doing.

## How to use this file

Work through these one at a time:
1. Tell me which numbered strategy to build next (or say "build the next one").
2. I'll write the full 6-section homepage for that strategy and tell you where to find it.
3. You review it in the app, we tweak or approve it, and I mark it ✅ below.
4. Repeat.

## 1. Problem-first

**Status:** ✅ Built & reviewed

**Core idea:** Make the reader feel the problem before any solution appears.

**For SpotShare:** Open cold on the bind itself — empty reserved spots, frustrated guests —
with no mention of SpotShare yet.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** State the problem only. No solution, no product name. Pure tension.
- **Problem:** Deepen and quantify the pain — what it actually costs residents, managers, the building.
- **How It Works:** First appearance of SpotShare. This is the relief moment — the turn from pain to fix.
- **Social Proof:** Show the pain disappearing for other buildings.
- **Audience Router:** "Whichever seat you're in, the pain looked like this — here's your fix."
- **Final CTA:** Direct, solution-confident close.

## 2. Outcome-first

**Status:** 🔄 In progress — built, awaiting your review

**Core idea:** Open with the resolved future state; explain the mechanism only after the reader wants it.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens on "guests just park" — the destination — withholding the "how."

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Paint the finished state vividly — guests parking effortlessly, zero friction.
- **Problem:** Brief contrast only — "here's what most buildings live with instead." Keep it short; this isn't the centerpiece.
- **How It Works:** This is where the mechanism finally gets explained.
- **Social Proof:** Other buildings who reached that outcome.
- **Audience Router:** "Here's what reaching that outcome looks like for your role."
- **Final CTA:** Forward-looking — "start reaching that outcome."

## 3. Story-first

**Status:** 🔄 In progress — built, awaiting your review

**Core idea:** Open with one concrete, specific scene and let everything else unfold from it.

**For SpotShare:** Hero is a single illustrative moment (e.g. a resident's parents driving in
for a visit), not an abstract claim about buildings in general.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** A single scene, present tense, specific sensory details.
- **Problem:** Zoom out from the story to the pattern — "this happens in buildings everywhere."
- **How It Works:** Return to the story's resolution — what would have happened with SpotShare.
- **Social Proof:** More mini-stories, plural — let illustrative voices carry this section.
- **Audience Router:** "Whose story is this — yours, or someone you manage?"
- **Final CTA:** Invite the reader to write their own version of the story.

## 4. Hero's Journey

**Status:** 🔄 In progress — built, awaiting your review

**Core idea:** The classic mythic structure — ordinary world, call to adventure, mentor/plan,
trials, transformation, return.

**For SpotShare:** Map building life directly onto the arc.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Ordinary world + call to adventure — life with the parking problem, and the moment something has to change.
- **Problem:** The struggle — what's already been tried and failed (paper passes, policy talk, manual workarounds).
- **How It Works:** The guide and the plan — SpotShare as the mentor handing over a clear method.
- **Social Proof:** Trials overcome — buildings that went through the same struggle and came out the other side.
- **Audience Router:** Choosing a path — resident / manager / board as three roads forward.
- **Final CTA:** The return, transformed — what life looks like after the journey.

## 5. Vision-first

**Status:** 🔄 In progress — built, awaiting your review

**Core idea:** Open with a big-picture belief about the future; position the product as part of getting there.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens on a future where buildings use space intelligently — not on the parking problem itself.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** State the future vision — shared, intelligent use of space in residential buildings.
- **Problem:** Name the gap between that vision and today's reality.
- **How It Works:** SpotShare as the bridge — the practical mechanism for reaching the vision.
- **Social Proof:** Evidence the vision is already happening elsewhere.
- **Audience Router:** "Your part in that future," by role.
- **Final CTA:** Invite the reader to be early to the vision.

## 6. Movement-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Frame adoption as joining a collective shift, not a private purchase decision.

**For SpotShare:** "Buildings everywhere are rethinking parking" — collective, momentum-driven language. "Join," not "buy."

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Name the shift already underway, invite the reader into it.
- **Problem:** Frame the old way (locked, unused spots) as what's being left behind.
- **How It Works:** What being part of the movement looks like in practice.
- **Social Proof:** A roll call of buildings already in — momentum signal.
- **Audience Router:** "However you show up to this movement" — role-based entry points.
- **Final CTA:** "Join" language, not "buy" language.

## 7. Contrarian-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Open by challenging a commonly-held assumption, then resolve the tension.

**For SpotShare:** "You don't have a parking shortage. You have a parking access problem."
Disrupt the reader's framing immediately.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** State the contrarian claim plainly, before any comfort or explanation.
- **Problem:** Justify the claim — why the common assumption is wrong.
- **How It Works:** Show the reframed solution that only makes sense once the premise is accepted.
- **Social Proof:** Buildings who believed the old story until they saw the results.
- **Audience Router:** Apply the reframe per role — what each audience got wrong, and what SpotShare corrects.
- **Final CTA:** Confident, assured close — "now you know."

---

## 8. Curiosity-gap

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Withhold a piece of information to create a pull toward the next section.

**For SpotShare:** Hero teases a fact or number without explaining it yet
("There's a number every building manager should know. Most don't.").

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Open a specific, unresolved question or teaser.
- **Problem:** Still circling the gap — building tension, not yet resolving it.
- **How It Works:** The reveal — close the loop opened in the hero.
- **Social Proof:** A second, smaller curiosity hook ("here's what happened next").
- **Audience Router:** Tease a role-specific detail per audience to pull them onward.
- **Final CTA:** One last small gap — "see what's available in your building."

## 9. Educational-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Teach something true and useful before selling anything.

**For SpotShare:** Open with a genuinely useful explanation of why reserved parking sits empty
(basic utilization economics), not a pitch.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** A teaching headline — a fact or insight, not a pitch.
- **Problem:** Explain the mechanism behind the problem like a short lesson.
- **How It Works:** The lesson naturally arrives at SpotShare as the applied answer.
- **Social Proof:** Evidence/data presented as further learning, not testimonial bragging.
- **Audience Router:** Role-specific "what this means for you" — still instructional in tone.
- **Final CTA:** Low-pressure, "learn more" framed close.

---

## 10. Proof-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Lead with evidence and credibility before narrative or persuasion.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens with a number, stat, or result, establishing trust immediately.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Headline built around a stat or proof point.
- **Problem:** Brief — supports the proof, doesn't need its own emotional buildup.
- **How It Works:** Plain mechanism explanation, still evidence-toned.
- **Social Proof:** The centerpiece of the page — give this the most space.
- **Audience Router:** Role-specific proof points.
- **Final CTA:** Confident, data-backed close.

---

## 11. Product-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Just show and explain what the product does, with minimal narrative framing.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens with what SpotShare literally is and does, plainly.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Plain product description — what it is, what it does.
- **Problem:** Brief context, secondary to the product explanation.
- **How It Works:** The heart of the page — mechanics, configuration, features.
- **Social Proof:** Functional proof — usage stats, reliability — not emotional testimonials.
- **Audience Router:** Feature-relevant routing — "the controls that matter to you."
- **Final CTA:** Direct, functional close ("see the product").

---

## 12. Experience-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Describe how it *feels* to use the product, rather than what it does or proves.

**For SpotShare:** Hero is written from the felt experience of a guest pulling in and parking immediately.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Sensory, present-moment description of the experience.
- **Problem:** The felt friction of today's experience, by contrast.
- **How It Works:** Described as a sequence of moments/feelings, not steps or features.
- **Social Proof:** First-person felt accounts, not stats.
- **Audience Router:** "What this feels like," by role.
- **Final CTA:** Inviting, experiential — "feel what this is like for your building."

## 13. Identity-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Open by naming who this is for, so the right reader self-selects immediately.

**For SpotShare:** Hero defines the kind of building or person this is built for, before
explaining anything.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** "If you're a building where X happens, this is for you" — identity framing.
- **Problem:** The problem as something that defines that identity ("you know this if you're one of these buildings").
- **How It Works:** The fix framed as something built specifically for that identity.
- **Social Proof:** Others like you / your kind of building.
- **Audience Router:** Identity sub-types — resident-identity, manager-identity, board-identity.
- **Final CTA:** "If that's you, here's the next step."

## 14. Philosophy-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Lead with the belief or principle behind the product, not features or pain.

**For SpotShare:** Hero states a belief — "space that sits empty is space that's failing the
people who own it" — before any product mention.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** State the guiding belief/principle plainly.
- **Problem:** Show how today's parking systems violate that principle.
- **How It Works:** SpotShare as the practical expression of the belief.
- **Social Proof:** Others who share or validate the belief.
- **Audience Router:** How the principle applies differently by role.
- **Final CTA:** Values-aligned close — "if you believe this too..."

## 15. Community-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Lead with neighbor-to-neighbor relationships and building culture before product mechanics.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens on residents helping each other, not on the product.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Neighbor-helping-neighbor framing, warm tone.
- **Problem:** The community cost of the current system — embarrassment, friction between neighbors.
- **How It Works:** Framed as a system for sharing, not just software.
- **Social Proof:** Community voices, building-culture anecdotes.
- **Audience Router:** "Your part in the community," by role.
- **Final CTA:** Warm, communal close — "bring this to your building."

---

## 16. Transformation-first

**Status:** ⬜ Not started

**Core idea:** Lead with before/after change — the throughline is the shift itself, not either steady state.

**For SpotShare:** Hero opens mid-transformation ("Before SpotShare... After SpotShare...")
rather than starting from problem or outcome alone.

**Build prompt:**
- **Hero:** Explicit before/after framing right away.
- **Problem:** The "before" expanded — fully felt.
- **How It Works:** The mechanism of the change itself — the turning point.
- **Social Proof:** Other buildings' before/after.
- **Audience Router:** Before/after, by role.
- **Final CTA:** "Make the shift" framed close.

## Implementation note

Each built strategy will live as a new entry in a `HOME_NARRATIVES` array in `src/data.ts`,
separate from the existing audience `PRESETS`. Each entry's `id` will match the kebab-case
strategy name used in this file (e.g. `heros-journey`, `curiosity-gap`) so the two stay easy
to cross-reference. A small selector will be added to the Home page sidebar to switch between
built narratives — this gets wired up when the first strategy is built, not before.
